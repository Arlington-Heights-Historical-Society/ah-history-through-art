# History through art project
Information for artists and members of the Art Guild community.

## Background context

In order to engage with our community and tell the stories in our collection, we propose to organize an event that tells stories using artistic expression, mostly through the creation of paintings, images and videos. The event will be the culmination of an open competition where artists can submit their work to tell the stories that inspire them.  
These stories will be constrained to two themes. The first is the story of Harmony Park. The second are stories from the HANA neighborhood and houses. 

## Themes

#### HANA theme
HANA stands for the Historic Arlington Neighborhood Association. It is a not for profit focused on retaining historic homes in the HANA neighboorhood boundaries.

The relationship home owners have with their homes.


#### Harmony park theme
[Peter and Mary Busch](https://www.idaillinois.org/digital/collection/narlingto04/id/114/rec/18) in 1899. 
The American house tavern was built by Peter and Mary Busch in the late 1800s and became a community staple. It changed owners a few times and in 1917 it was renamed to the Vail. It remained the vail until it burned down in 1988 (March 8?). Many people mourned the passing of a beloved gathering place. In 2005 Harmony Park was constructed on its lot. Today summer concert series take place in the park continuing the traditional of being a staple of the downtown area. [The location](https://maps.app.goo.gl/DTaVPmeUzkxobbs3A) downtown.
